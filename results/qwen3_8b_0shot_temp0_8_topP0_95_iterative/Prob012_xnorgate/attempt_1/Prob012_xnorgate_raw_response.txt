To implement an XNOR gate in Verilog, we leverage the fact that the XNOR operation is the logical negation of the XOR operation. The XOR of two inputs, `a` and `b`, is `a ^ b`, and the XNOR is simply the inverse of that, which can be expressed as `~(a ^ b)`.

This approach is concise, efficient, and directly reflects the logical behavior of the XNOR gate.
